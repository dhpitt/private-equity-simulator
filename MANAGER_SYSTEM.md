# Manager System

## Overview

The Manager System brings management teams to life with:
1. **Visible Impact**: Quarterly narratives showing how manager attributes affect performance
2. **Strategic Hiring**: Choose between multiple candidates with different strengths and tradeoffs
3. **Narrative Events**: Detailed stories for hiring and firing managers

- **Medium (50-75%)**: Solid execution, maintains steady operations

## Strategic Implications

### When to Replace Management

**Strong Reasons:**
- Competence < 50% (poor performer)
- Low cooperativeness causing friction (< 40%)
- Company underperforming consistently
- Need specific skillset (e.g., turnaround artist for struggling company)

**Weak Reasons:**
- Marginal competence improvement (60% â†’ 65% not worth it)
- One bad quarter (variance happens)
- Just hired recently (transition costs add up)

### Choosing the Right Candidate

**For a Healthy, Growing Company:**
â†’ **The Operator** (steady execution)

**For a Struggling Company:**
â†’ **The Turnaround Artist** (aggressive fixes)

**For Maximum Growth Potential:**
â†’ **The Visionary** (high risk, high reward)

**For Maximum Performance:**
â†’ **The Star** (but prepare for conflict)

**For Maximum Control:**
â†’ **The Yes-Person** (but limited upside)

**For Low Drama:**
â†’ **The Safe Choice** (solid B+ performer)

### Tradeoffs Are Real

You **cannot** get a manager with:
- 95% competence + 95% cooperativeness + low risk
- High competence + high cooperativeness = lower risk tolerance
- Very high competence + very high cooperativeness = rare and expensive

**Every candidate has tradeoffs:**
- The Star won't listen to you
- The Yes-Person isn't very competent
- The Visionary might blow up
- The Turnaround Artist is aggressive (not gentle)

### Transition Costs

**Every manager replacement incurs:**

1. **Direct Cost**: $500K
2. **Transition Penalty**: -1% to -3% growth (temporary)
3. **Volatility Change**: Based on risk profile difference
4. **Potential Additional Costs**: If old manager was uncooperative
5. **Reputation Hit**: If termination is difficult

**This means:**
- Don't churn managers every quarter
- Make sure the upgrade is worth it
- Time replacements strategically (not right before exit)
